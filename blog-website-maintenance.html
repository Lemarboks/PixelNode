Maintenance

Do you really need monthly website maintenance?

Written by Lemar Boks · PixelNode

A website isn't a "build it and forget it" thing. Once it's live, it needs looking after — but how much, and is a monthly plan worth it? Here's a straight answer.

What website maintenance actually covers

  • Hosting & uptime — keeping the site online, fast, and reachable.
  • Backups — regular, automated copies so nothing is lost if something breaks.
  • Updates — content changes, security patches, and small improvements.
  • Monitoring & support — catching problems early and having someone to call when you need a change.

Do you need it?

If your website matters to your business — if customers find you through it, contact you through it, or buy through it — then yes. An unmaintained site slowly degrades: things break, security lapses, and content goes stale. The cost of fixing a neglected site later is usually higher than maintaining it along the way.

Why a care plan beats one-off fixes

Paying a separate fee every time you need a change adds up — and it makes you hesitate to ask for the small improvements that keep a site healthy. A monthly care plan bundles hosting, backups, updates, and support into one predictable cost, so the site stays looked after without a fee on every request.

PixelNode's care plans start at R350/month, scaling up to include content updates, priority support, and monthly improvements.

The bottom line

If your website earns its keep, maintenance isn't an optional extra — it's insurance plus upkeep. A small monthly cost protects a much bigger investment.
